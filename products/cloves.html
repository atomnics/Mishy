The Origin Story

Zanzibar, once known as the "Spice Islands," has been cultivating cloves since the early 19th century. The archipelago's tropical climate, rich volcanic soil, and ocean breezes create perfect growing conditions for these aromatic flower buds that have driven global trade for centuries.

Our partner farmers on Pemba and Unguja islands maintain traditional harvesting methods, hand-picking each bud at the precise moment of peak oil concentration—a skill passed down through generations of spice farming families.

Aromatic Profile

Warm & Sweet Peppery Woody Slightly Bitter Medicinal

Zanzibar cloves are renowned for their exceptional eugenol content, delivering an intensely aromatic profile with warm, sweet undertones and a subtle peppery finish. The essential oil extracted from these cloves is prized in pharmaceuticals, perfumery, and premium food applications.
