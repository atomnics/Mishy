The Origin Story

The Njombe region, perched at 1,800-2,200 meters in Tanzania's Southern Highlands, offers a unique microclimate that produces avocados of exceptional quality. Cool temperatures, high altitude, and rich volcanic soils combine to create fruit with remarkably high dry matter content—a key indicator of premium taste and texture.

Tanzania's counter-seasonal harvest (March-September) positions Njombe avocados perfectly to supply European and Middle Eastern markets during their off-season, commanding premium prices for fresh, air-freighted fruit.

Quality Characteristics

Creamy Texture Nutty Flavor High Oil Content Slow Ripening

Njombe Hass avocados are prized for their buttery, creamy flesh and rich, nutty flavor profile. The high altitude slows fruit development, concentrating oils and producing avocados that ripen slowly and evenly—ideal for long-distance transport to premium markets.
